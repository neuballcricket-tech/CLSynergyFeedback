Flow 1 — Customer Inquiry to Quotation

Changed
Customer
Sends inquiry
Customer asks for a rate or booking by email, WhatsApp, or phone.
→
Sales / CS
New or existing customer?
New customers need KYC checks first. Existing customers move faster.
→
CS / Sales
Log inquiry + set priority
Inquiry is logged with origin, destination, commodity, container type, and special equipment needs. Priority level is assigned.
▲ Priority can be set at creation and changed mid-workflow by Sales or CS. Commodity type and special equipment (open top, flat rack, reefer) now captured at inquiry level.
→
CS / Finance
KYC / background check
For new customers, Finance reviews KYC. Existing approved customers skip this.
→
CS / Sales
Multi-step rate check
Rates checked across multiple sources: AMS contracted/monthly rates → spot rates (liner portal / INTTRA) → volume-based rates → NAC rates (restricted). Best available options collected.
▲ Now a multi-source check: AMS for contracted rates, liner portals / INTTRA for real-time spot rates, volume-based rates where applicable, NAC rates for named accounts (restricted to assigned salesperson + procurement). Sales has full access to spot rates alongside CS.
→
CS / Sales / Procurement
Rate available? Escalate?
If no rate found, escalate to Procurement. Even if rates exist, can still escalate for better options or spot rate opportunities.
▲ Escalation to Procurement is always optional — even when rates exist. Procurement can also proactively surface spot rates that beat contracted rates.
→
Sales / CS
Send multiple quotation options
Multiple liner options compiled and sent to the customer. Customer selects preferred liner/rate. Sent via email or WhatsApp. Can include a mix of contracted, spot, and volume-based rates from different liners.
▲ Not a single quote — multiple liner options sent so the customer can choose. Both Sales and CS can send. Procurement can manually add spot options alongside system-generated ones.
Rate types & checking sequence Changed
1. Contracted rate — Monthly or quarterly validity, from liner agreements. Procurement maintains in AMS. Checked first.
2. Spot rate — 24 hours to vessel-specific validity. Changes every minute for some liners. Checked at inquiry AND booking — everyone (Sales, CS, Procurement) should have real-time access. Not maintained in AMS.
3. Volume-based rate — Based on shipment volume commitments. Not maintained in AMS — needs new system support.
4. NAC rate — Special rates for ~10 named accounts per trade lane. Valid 6–12 months. Restricted visibility: only assigned salesperson + procurement. No price-request process needed.
5. China rates — Vessel-by-vessel, overnight changes. Different from the European monthly model.
Rate Maintenance workflow New Flow
Liner publishes rate — via email, WhatsApp commercial group, or liner portal. Contracted rates monthly/quarterly, spot rates change every minute for some liners.
↓
Pricing team receives & enters — currently manual via Excel. System must support Excel bulk upload for rate ingestion and manual entry fallback.
↓
INTTRA / WiseTech auto-pull — spot rates and schedules auto-populated via middleware (available within ~4 months). Removes manual entry for supported liners.
↓
Rate validated & categorized — tagged as contracted, spot, volume-based, or NAC. Commodity premiums (food, hazardous) and container-size rates (20ft vs 40ft) applied.
↓
Rate available in system — searchable by CS, Sales, Procurement. NAC rates restricted. Spot rates visible to all (Sales, CS, Procurement).
↓
Key gap: Spot rates and volume-based rates are NOT in AMS today. No structured database exists yet. Rate database architecture is the biggest unresolved item.
Meeting decision: Surcharges should be presented as all-in rates (base + surcharges combined) — CLS does not need an itemized surcharge breakdown on the quotation.
Customer master creation — duplicate prevention New
New customer request — CS or Sales initiates customer registration via workspace or command center
↓
Duplicate check — system performs fuzzy match on company name, address, contact person, email, phone. If potential duplicate found, user is prompted to link to existing record instead of creating new
↓
Customer details captured — name, email, address, telephone, contact person, customer type (shipper/buyer/agent/trader). Linked to salesperson assignment
↓
Finance KYC approval — submitted to Finance for background/credit check. Finance approves or rejects. No inquiry can proceed until Finance approves
↓
Customer code generated — master record created in system. Synced to GenSoft and AMS. Customer available for future inquiries without re-registration
Duplicate risk & current gaps New
Problem: Same customer can be registered multiple times across AMS, GenSoft, and new system if no cross-system check exists
Problem: Different salespeople may independently register the same company under slightly different names (e.g. "CL Synergy" vs "CLS" vs "C.L. Synergy Pvt Ltd")
Solution: Single point of customer creation with fuzzy matching. All downstream systems (GenSoft, AMS, CL Connect) reference the same master ID
Solution: Finance approval gate prevents unapproved customers from entering any workflow. Approval status visible to CS and Sales

Flow 2 — Customer Response, Booking, and Confirmation

Changed
Customer
Accepts / rejects quote
Customer confirms by WhatsApp, email, or phone call — often just "confirm CMA option."
→
Sales / CS
Record customer response
Either Sales or CS can record the acceptance or rejection in the system.
▲ Both Sales and CS can record responses. Customers contact whichever team they have a relationship with.
→
CS
Prepare booking request
CS compiles booking details (customer, containers, RA number if applicable) and sends to Procurement.
▲ RA numbers must be included for contract-rate bookings. Data carries forward from the inquiry — no re-entry needed.
→
Procurement
Review + edit before submission
Procurement reviews all booking details on an editable screen. Verifies RA numbers, container details, and makes amendments before pushing to the liner.
★ New editable checkpoint. Not one-click — Procurement must review and can amend before liner submission.
→
Procurement → Liner
Submit booking to liner
Booking submitted via INTTRA middleware or liner portal. Manual email fallback for liners without online systems (PIL, some Indian lines).
▲ INTTRA integration planned as primary path. Manual/email fallback always maintained.
→
Liner
Booking confirmed
Shipping line confirms space on the vessel and assigns vessel/voyage.
→
System
Notify Sales + CS
Automatic notification sent to assigned salesperson, CS team, and procurement that booking is confirmed.
★ New: auto-notification to all three parties. Customer is NOT informed yet — only after Release Order.
→
Procurement / CS
Issue Release Order
Formal release order prepared and sent to customer. This is the customer-facing confirmation.
▲ Clarified: there can be hours to days between liner confirmation and release order. Customer only notified at this step.
Normal booking path Updated
Customer confirms quotation
↓
Sales or CS records response
↓
CS sends booking request to Procurement
↓
Procurement reviews on editable screen — verifies RA#, containers, amends if needed
↓
Procurement submits to liner (INTTRA or portal or email)
↓
Liner confirms → auto-notify Sales + CS
↓
Release Order issued to customer
Spot booking — with inquiry Updated
Spot rate identified as better than contract rate
↓
Sales, CS, or Procurement can initiate spot booking
↓
Procurement makes the final booking decision on cost
↓
Booking placed → notify all parties
↓
Risk: cancellation charges apply if booking is unused
Speculative spot booking — no inquiry New
Market spot rate significantly below current rate (e.g. $1,000 less)
↓
Procurement proactively books space without a linked inquiry
↓
Sales team notified to find customers to fill the space
↓
Booking linked to an inquiry later, or cancelled if unused
↓
Cancellation charges apply — this is a commercial risk decision
Contract space / slot utilization tracking New
Total contracted space — contracted TEU/container allocation per trade lane per period, pulled from liner agreements
↓
Confirmed bookings — bookings placed against contracted space. System tracks count at booking level (e.g. 500 of 1,000 TEU confirmed)
↓
Actual containers shipped (BL level) — variance tracked at BL stage. If 5 containers booked but only 4 shipped, difference logged
↓
"Balance to sell" — remaining capacity visible to Sales. Salesperson can follow up with customer on unused containers or find new cargo
↓
Risk visibility: CL Synergy-initiated speculative bookings vs customer-requested bookings must be tracked separately. Cancellation charge exposure and fill rate reported to management
New requirement — Booking amendments & utilization: After a booking is placed, customers may request container quantity changes (e.g. 5→10 containers). The system must support amendments to existing bookings, not just new bookings. Container variance tracking is needed: if 5 booked but only 4 shipped, the difference is logged at BL level and visible to Sales as "balance to sell." Contract space utilization (contracted vs booked vs shipped) must be reportable per trade lane, per liner, per period — see Flow 8 for reporting details.

Flow 3 — Cut-offs, SI, and Liner Portal Entry

Changed
Liner
Sends cut-off schedule
Deadlines for SI, BL, VGM, gate-in, etc. Same vessel can have different cut-offs for different carriers.
→
System
Track cut-offs + auto-reminders
Cut-off dates stored in the system. Automated reminders sent 2 days before each deadline.
★ Replaces manual Excel tracker. 2-day auto-reminders for SI, BL, and all cut-offs — currently done manually every day.
→
CS
Remind customer for SI
CS asks customer to send SI. SI typically due 4 days before EDI cut-off.
→
Customer
Send SI
Customer sends shipment details via PDF, Excel, Word, email, or plain text. System accepts all formats.
→
CS / Docs
House BL check — edit checkpoint
If customer takes a House BL: shipper, consignee, and delivery agent must be changed before liner submission. If Liner BL only: can go direct.
★ Conditional checkpoint: House BL requires editing (CLS becomes shipper, agent becomes consignee). Liner BL goes straight through.
→
CS / Docs
Submit SI to liner
SI entered into liner portal or submitted via INTTRA. Email fallback for liners without online systems.
▲ Some liners (Maersk) may reject INTTRA and require direct portal entry. Some (PIL, Indian lines) have no online system — email only.
→
Liner
BL draft generated
Liner sends a draft BL for checking and correction.
New requirement — AMS filing for US-bound shipments: Documentation must be filed with US Customs 48–72 hours before vessel arrival at the first US port. This filing deadline needs tracking and reminders in the system, separate from SI/BL cut-offs.

↓
One container, one Master BL

GenSoft handoff: The new system handles the workflow up to BL creation. From BL stage onward, data is replicated in GenSoft for job creation, invoicing, costing, and document management. API integration with GenSoft is planned to avoid duplicate entry. Documentation team works primarily in GenSoft for HBL creation and customer invoicing.
Vessel creation & tracking New
Booking confirmed — vessel/voyage assigned by liner at booking confirmation
↓
Check if vessel exists — system checks if vessel + voyage + POD (port of discharge) combo already exists in GenSoft. Same vessel/voyage can serve multiple bookings
↓
If new: create vessel record — currently manual in GenSoft. Vessel created per trade lane, per voyage, per POD. One-time creation — others reuse it
↓
INTTRA integration (target) — vessel schedules, ETD/ETA, and voyage data should be auto-pulled from INTTRA to eliminate manual vessel creation. Tracking updates also via INTTRA
↓
Job creation proceeds — once vessel record exists, documentation team can create jobs in GenSoft without re-entering vessel details
Reporting / list creation New
Pending BL drafts — list of BLs awaiting customer approval, sortable by cut-off urgency
↓
Amendment tracking — BL correction cycles logged. How many rounds per customer, average time to finalize
↓
Finance approval queue — BLs held for credit-related or payment approval. Visible to Finance and Docs teams
↓
Filing deadlines — US AMS filings, EBL requirements, cut-off compliance. Queryable via Command Center
EBL readiness (2028): European boards will mandate Electronic Bills of Lading from 2028 — €65 penalty per paper BL. The INTTRA module supports MBL functions for EBL. WiseTech/Citra/E2O platforms being evaluated for integration.

New
Sales customer assignment New
Each salesperson has assigned customers — only they can see those customers' inquiries and data
↓
Other salespeople cannot access another salesperson's customers
↓
One customer can be handled by multiple salespeople in different contexts (e.g. nomination shipments from agents)
↓
Reassignment option available when a salesperson is on leave — backup person gets temporary access
CS and Documentation access New
CS team has full access to all customers across all salespeople
↓
Export Documentation team: full visibility within the team, but editing restricted to assigned person's work
↓
Doc team access: job creation, HBL, invoicing, costing in GenSoft
↓
Backup assignment: when someone is on leave, specific team members can be given temporary edit permission
Named Account rate visibility New
~10 key-account customers have special negotiated rates per trade lane
↓
Rates valid 6–12 months, no price-request process needed
↓
Visible ONLY to assigned salesperson + Procurement. Not visible to other sales or CS.
↓

Flow 7 — Systems and Integration Architecture

Changed
New Platform
CL Connect (Inquiry → BL)
Handles inquiry, quotation, booking, SI, and BL workflows. Primary operating interface for CS, Sales, and Procurement.
★ This is the new system being built — replaces Excel trackers, WhatsApp coordination, and fragmented email workflows.
→
Middleware
INTTRA / WiseTech
Middleware for liner connectivity — rates, schedules, bookings, SI submission, and vessel tracking. Recently acquired by WiseTech (CargoWise parent). Spot rates available within ~4 months.
▲ Primary integration path. Vessel schedules, ETD/ETA, and voyage data to be auto-pulled to eliminate manual vessel creation in GenSoft. Not all liners supported — manual fallback always required.
→
Liner portals
Carrier booking and SI
Fallback for liners not on INTTRA or when INTTRA has technical issues. Some liners (Maersk) may prefer direct portal over INTTRA.
+
GenSoft
Jobs, House BL, invoicing
Existing ERP. Handles job creation, HBL, costing, customer invoicing from BL stage onward. API integration planned.
+
AMS
Rate database (current)
Current rate and customer system. Will be replaced/absorbed by the new platform's rate module.
Communication channels Updated
WhatsApp Business API — Individual employee accounts connected to the system. Not a general company number.
Email — Individual work email connected per employee. System can send quotations, confirmations, reminders through connected email.
WhatsApp commercial group — Existing internal group for rate updates and trade information. Will be integrated alongside the system interface — both kept for transition period.
Customer portal (future) — Option for customers to accept/reject quotations via a secure link without logging in. Kept as optional, not mandatory.
Multi-office operations New
Colombo is the hub — entire workflow for all global offices happens here
Overseas offices (India, Australia, Dubai, UK, US) focus on sales activities
Operations, documentation, procurement, and finance are centralized in Colombo
System currently scoped for Colombo only, but architecture should support multi-office expansion

Flow 8 — Command Center, Reporting & Analytics

New
All Roles
Command Center
Chat-based interface for natural language queries. Role-specific quick commands: new inquiry, follow up, pending items. Users type queries like "what are my pending jobs" and get structured results.
★ One of three primary operating interfaces alongside Dashboard and Workspace. Each role (CS, Sales, Procurement, Docs) gets role-specific quick commands and query access.
→
System
List generation
Queries return structured lists: pending inquiries, overdue SIs, upcoming cut-offs, bookings awaiting confirmation, BLs pending approval. Filterable by date, customer, salesperson, trade lane.
→
All Roles
Reports & exports
Lists exportable as reports. KPIs configurable per role. Pending jobs, quotation conversion rates, booking fill rates, cut-off compliance — all queryable.
→
Management
Dashboard KPIs
Role-customized dashboards. CS sees pending inquiries and SI status. Sales sees quotation pipeline and customer activity. Procurement sees booking fill rates and contract utilization. Management sees cross-team overview.
★ Dashboards configurable — KPIs and metrics can be changed per role without development.
Command Center quick commands New
Customer Service: new customer, new inquiry, follow up on inquiry, pending SI reminders, cut-off status
Sales: my pending quotations, customer activity this week, balance to sell, named account status
Procurement: pending booking requests, contract utilization %, spot rate opportunities, liner performance
Documentation: pending BL drafts, amendment cycles, filing deadlines (AMS/EBL), jobs awaiting vessel creation
Analytics & tracking New
Contract utilization — contracted space vs confirmed bookings vs actual shipped. By trade lane, by liner, by period
Booking variance — booked containers vs actual containers at BL level. "Balance to sell" per booking visible to Sales
Speculative booking risk — CL Synergy-initiated bookings: fill rate, cancellation charges incurred, revenue impact
Quotation pipeline — inquiries → quotations sent → accepted → booked → shipped. Conversion at each stage, by salesperson
AI feature (optional): Natural language processing in Command Center allows free-text queries. Level of AI integration to be decided — can start with quick commands and structured queries, with natural language as an enhancement layer.
